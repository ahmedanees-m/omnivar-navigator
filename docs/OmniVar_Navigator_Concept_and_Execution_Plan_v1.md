# OmniVar Navigator — Concept Brief & Execution Plan (v1.0, draft)

**Working name:** OmniVar Navigator (engine codename: *ARGO* — "navigating the diagnostic odyssey"; name is a placeholder, open to change)
**Scope decision:** Broad, full–post-negative-workup engine across all modalities (not a fixed gene-panel tool)
**Priority clinical domain:** Inherited bleeding & platelet disorders (with epilepsy and cancer-predisposition as the next domain packs)
**Status:** Concept brief + phased roadmap for review and revision
**Date:** 2026-06-11

> This document replaces the prior "OmniVar-Q" plan. The quantum module is removed. The center of gravity moves from "another variant interpreter" to a **rule-grounded decision and orchestration engine that turns a VUS from a dead-end into a costed, auditable resolution path** — and, more broadly, decides the optimal next step in the entire post-negative diagnostic workup.

---

## PART I — CONCEPT BRIEF

### 1. One-line definition

OmniVar Navigator is a **rule-grounded, agentic clinical-decision-support engine** that sits on top of existing variant-prioritization and pathogenicity tools and answers a question none of them answer: *"For this specific unsolved patient and this specific uncertain variant, what is the single most informative, cost-effective next action — and what would it do to the classification?"*

It is a **decision-support** system, not an autonomous classifier: it advises the geneticist or multidisciplinary team (MDT), shows its full reasoning, and keeps the human in control of every call.

### 2. The problem

Three facts define the gap we are attacking:

1. **The odyssey is long and mostly unsolved.** Rare disease affects ~300 million people; the diagnostic rate after standard exome/genome sequencing is only ~25–30%, and the average time from symptom onset to diagnosis is ~6 years. The majority of cases remain unsolved even after sequencing.
2. **What actually resolves hard cases is *evidence acquisition*, not better ranking.** The unsolved tail is cracked by *multimodal escalation* — RNA-seq / targeted long-read RNA, long-read genome, optical genome mapping, methylation episignatures, proteomics, trio/segregation, and targeted functional assays — plus continual reinterpretation. Studies show a tailored stepwise modality choice can resolve a large fraction of previously unsolved cases, **but there is explicitly "no consensus on the optimal diagnostic care pathway after negative first-line results."** That decision is currently made by hand, case-by-case, in weekly expert case conferences.
3. **A VUS is, formally, an evidence deficit.** Under the ClinGen/Tavtigian Bayesian point adaptation of ACMG/AMP, every piece of evidence carries calibrated points (Supporting/Moderate/Strong/Very Strong = ±1/±2/±4/±8). A variant is a **VUS precisely because its accumulated points sit in the uncertain band** — short of the threshold in either direction. Resolving it means closing that points gap.

**The unmet need:** Every existing tool — in-silico predictors (AlphaMissense, REVEL, CADD), prioritizers (Exomiser, AI-MARRVEL, LIRICAL), agentic differential-diagnosis systems (DeepRare), single-criterion automators (autoPVS1, AutoPM3), and full classifiers (InterVar, BIAS-2015, and the commercial platforms Emedgene, Franklin, eVai, Varsome) — **classifies or ranks from the evidence already available.** None decides *what new evidence to go acquire* to break a stuck case or VUS, none prices that decision, and none corrects for the fact that the evidence it leans on is ancestry-biased. ClinGen itself ran a one-off algorithmic analysis of how added functional evidence *could* reclassify VUS — proving the logic is sound — but nobody has turned it into a live, per-patient decision engine.

### 3. The solution

OmniVar Navigator reframes the diagnostic odyssey as a **sequential decision problem under uncertainty and budget**, operating in the native language of variant classification (evidence codes and points). For each case it:

1. **Builds a belief state** by orchestrating the best existing tools as evidence sources (it does not re-implement them).
2. **Maintains a rule-grounded, auditable points ledger** for each candidate variant using the formal ClinGen/VCEP criteria — the classification is computed *deterministically*, never generated by a language model.
3. **Performs evidence-gap analysis:** identifies which missing evidence codes are *attainable* for this variant, and how many points each would add to cross a threshold.
4. **Maps each attainable code to a concrete real-world action** (e.g., functional assay → PS3/BS3; RNA-seq → PS3 splicing/BS3; confirm de novo → PS2; family segregation → PP1/BS4; more cases/case-control → PS4/PM3; specific phenotype → PP4).
5. **Ranks actions by expected information gain per dollar and per week** (a value-of-information formulation calibrated on real conditional yields), and **recommends the cheapest path to resolution** — with calibrated uncertainty and a full audit trail.
6. **Closes the loop:** when results return, it updates the ledger and re-classifies, and the human's accept/override plus the realized outcome become clean, rule-anchored training signal.

At the whole-case level (the broad scope), the same machinery operates one level up: the action space includes whole-modality escalations (which omic assay next), reanalysis timing, and submission to matchmaking — so the engine advises the optimal next step across the *entire* post-negative workup, not just single-variant resolution.

The genuinely novel contributions are the **value-of-information decision layer over ACMG evidence codes**, the **rule-grounded (neurosymbolic) architecture** that makes an agent safe enough for clinical use, the **built-in equity mechanism**, and the **verifiable learning loop** — not the orchestration plumbing itself.

### 5. System architecture

A neurosymbolic, layered "conductor" design. The agent does the *soft* work (reading, retrieving, deciding, explaining); a deterministic rule engine does the *classification*.

**Design principles**

- **Conductor over the *open* ecosystem.** Every external tool sits behind a swappable adapter interface. Closed commercial platforms (Emedgene, Franklin) are treated as *peers/benchmarks*, never dependencies. No primary result hard-depends on a proprietary API.
- **Deterministic verdicts.** The LLM/agent never outputs the classification. It proposes evidence; the rule engine computes points. This directly answers the documented LLM-hallucination problem in variant classification.
- **Everything auditable.** Each evidence code traces to its source, strength, and the VCEP rule version applied; the points math is reproducible; the recommendation logs its inputs.
- **Graceful degradation.** When no attainable evidence exists, the honest output is "not currently resolvable — recommend reanalysis in N months / matchmaking," which is itself a useful, defensible answer.

### 6. The equity mechanism (concrete, not aspirational)

Documented fact: non-European-ancestry patients receive VUS results 1.5–2× more often, and the disparity is *driven by* the allele-frequency (PM2/BS1) and in-silico (PP3/BP4) evidence codes being trained on European-skewed data — while functional (MAVE/PS3/BS3) evidence is ancestry-equitable. Naively building another predictor on today's data therefore *worsens* equity.

OmniVar Navigator turns this into a mechanism:

1. **Ancestry inference** from the VCF (genetic-similarity grouping, not self-report).
2. **Evidence-reliability flags:** when PM2/PP3 are being applied to a patient from an under-represented group with sparse reference data, the engine down-weights or annotates their reliability and surfaces the uncertainty explicitly.
3. **Equitable-evidence routing:** in evidence-gap analysis, the engine *preferentially recommends* ancestry-robust next actions (functional assays, family segregation) over actions that would only add unreliable points.
4. **Equity reporting:** per-case and cohort-level dashboards quantify how much of each classification rests on ancestry-biased vs. equitable evidence.

This is also locally salient: inherited bleeding and platelet disorders are enriched in consanguineous and under-represented populations (e.g., South Asia, Middle East), where novel-variant rates are high and database representation is low — exactly where an equity-aware engine matters most.

### 7. Priority domain — inherited bleeding & platelet disorders

Bleeding disorders are an unusually strong first domain because the **rule infrastructure and the functional-assay ecosystem the engine depends on already exist and are mature**:

**Gene-specific rules (machine-readable, versioned on ClinGen's CSpec registry):**

- **F8, F9** — Hemophilia A/B — *ClinGen Coagulation Factor Deficiency VCEP (CFD-VCEP)*; F8 specification already updated with RNA/splicing (PVS1, PP3/BP4/BP7) and hemizygote (PS4) rules.
- **VWF** — von Willebrand disease — *ClinGen VWD VCEP*; subtype-aware (Types 1, 2A/2B/2M/2N, 3) with functional and phenotype (PP4) criteria.
- **ENG, ACVRL1** — Hereditary Hemorrhagic Telangiectasia — *ClinGen HHT VCEP*.
- **ITGA2B, ITGB3** — Glanzmann thrombasthenia (platelet integrin αIIbβ3) — the "integrin/bleeding" sub-domain.
- **GP1BA, GP1BB, GP9** — Bernard-Soulier syndrome; plus rare factor deficiencies (F2, F5, F7, F10, F11, F13, FGA/FGB/FGG) and other inherited platelet disorders.

**Functional/orthogonal assays the engine can recommend (mapped to PS3/BS3 and splicing PS3):**
factor activity assays; VWF:FVIII binding, VWF multimer analysis, ristocetin cofactor activity; light-transmission aggregometry (LTA); flow cytometry for platelet glycoproteins (CD41/CD61 for αIIbβ3, CD42 for GPIb); clot retraction; PFA closure time; thrombin generation; and RNA-seq / RT-PCR for splicing.

**Two worked examples that show why this engine matters:**

- *Synonymous-but-not-silent (Glanzmann).* A homozygous **synonymous** ITGB3 variant looks benign to every in-silico missense predictor, yet disrupts splicing and is pathogenic. An evidence-gap engine recognizes that the only path out of VUS is **RNA evidence (RT-PCR/RNA-seq → PS3 splicing)** and recommends it directly — instead of leaving the variant stranded under misleadingly benign in-silico scores.
- *Phenotype + functional stacking (VWD type 2N).* A VWF missense VUS reaches Pathogenic when the engine assembles **PS3 (functional: decreased VWF:FVIII binding) + PM3 + PP4 (type-2N-specific phenotype) + PP1 (segregation)** — and the engine can tell the team, up front, that *VWF:FVIII binding* is the single highest-yield assay to commission.

### 8. Scope and expansion path

The full-odyssey concept is **domain-agnostic**; each clinical domain is a "pack" of (a) gene-specific rule specifications, (b) an assay/modality catalog with cost/turnaround/yield priors, and (c) phenotype mappings.

- **Tier 1 (v1 priority): Bleeding & platelet disorders** — F8, F9, VWF, ITGA2B/ITGB3, GP1BA/B/9, rare factor deficiencies, ENG/ACVRL1.
- **Tier 2: Epilepsy / neurodevelopmental** — SCN1A, SCN2A, KCNQ2, CACNA1A, STXBP1, etc. (large VUS burden; strong phenotype signal; ion-channel functional assays / patch-clamp).
- **Tier 3: Cancer predisposition** — BRCA1/2, mismatch-repair/Lynch (MLH1, MSH2, etc.), TP53, PTEN, CDH1 — domains with mature VCEPs *and* abundant MAVE data, ideal for demonstrating the functional-evidence routing and equity mechanisms.

The whole-odyssey (cross-modality) layer is built domain-agnostically in Tier 1 and simply inherits new packs as domains are added.

### 9. Validation strategy

The central scientific claim is **separable and testable**: *the decision/orchestration layer adds diagnostic yield and/or reduces cost-and-time-to-resolution over using the same underlying tools without it.* Validation proceeds on four fronts:

1. **The core ablation (headline result):** run the full system vs. the same pipeline with the decision layer removed (i.e., the underlying tools alone), on the same cohorts. Metrics: VUS resolution rate, evidence points gained, cost per resolved case, time-to-resolution, concordance with expert/VCEP ground truth.
2. **Retrospective "diagnostic journey" validation:** use cohorts where the *sequence of actions and the eventual resolution* is known — Solve-RD / RD-Connect, UDN, and bleeding-specific registries/expert datasets (e.g., EAHAD/ISTH-curated variant sets, ClinGen eRepo bleeding-gene classifications). Test whether the engine's recommended next action matches the action that actually resolved the case, and at lower predicted cost.
3. **Simulation harness (for controlled stress-testing and ablation power):** a parametric simulator (carried over and adapted from the prior plan) drawing assay sensitivities/yields and costs from published literature, to generate odysseys with known ground-truth resolution paths. Used to test the VOI policy against greedy and random baselines.
4. **Calibration + equity validation:** reliability diagrams for the engine's confidence; ancestry-stratified analysis showing (a) the engine flags ancestry-biased evidence correctly and (b) equitable-evidence routing reduces the gap in resolution rate between ancestry groups.

**Prospective validation** (shadow-mode deployment alongside a real MDT, comparing recommended vs. actual next actions and outcomes) is the eventual gold standard and is scoped as a later phase / Paper 2 — v1 honestly demonstrates the framework, calibration, and retrospective/simulated yield, not yet prospective clinical superiority.

### 10. Risks and honest limitations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Needs *outcome* data (which action resolved which case), harder to get than static labels | Retrospective journey cohorts (Solve-RD/UDN/bleeding registries) + simulation; prospective deferred to Paper 2 |
| "It's just a wrapper" reviewer dismissal | Lead with the decision theory, rule-grounded calibration, equity mechanism, learning loop — those are the science; orchestration is substrate |
| Dependency/reproducibility (tools archived or APIs change — cf. SpliceAI) | Swappable adapter interfaces; pinned/containerized; no primary result depends on a closed API |
| Garbage-in from a wrong upstream tool | Cross-tool disagreement is a *feature*: it triggers human-review flags rather than silent acceptance |
| Some VUS are genuinely unresolvable at current science | Honest "not currently resolvable" output + reanalysis/matchmaking recommendation |
| Cost/yield priors are uncertain and lab-specific | Priors are explicit, versioned, and updated by the learning loop per institution |
| Modest absolute reclassification rates (~25% in some domains) | We promise an *optimized, transparent, equitable path*, not a miracle; report results honestly including negatives |

## PART II — EXECUTION PLAN & PHASED ROADMAP

Each step is written as **Objective · Goal · Methods · Code/artifacts · Expected outcome.** Code artifacts are named modules with key functions and (for the novel pieces) illustrative pseudocode; they are specifications, not final implementations.

### Phase 0 — Foundations & rule engine (Months 1–3)

**Step 0.1 — Project + reproducibility skeleton**
- **Objective:** A reproducible, vendor-neutral codebase.
- **Goal:** One-command environment + CI; adapter pattern established.
- **Methods:** Containerized environment (pinned deps); pytest + CI; pluggable `Adapter` abstract base class so every external tool is swappable.
- **Code/artifacts:** `core/adapter.py` (ABC: `fetch(variant, patient) -> EvidenceContribution`), `pyproject.toml`, `Dockerfile`, `.github/workflows/ci.yml`.
- **Expected outcome:** Green CI; a "hello-world" adapter passes contract tests.

**Step 0.2 — Rule engine (deterministic ClinGen/Tavtigian point system)**
- **Objective:** A deterministic classifier from evidence codes → classification.
- **Goal:** Implement the Bayesian point system and the combining rules, parameterized by gene-specific VCEP specifications.
- **Methods:** Encode evidence strengths as points (Supporting/Moderate/Strong/Very-Strong = 1/2/4/8; benign negative); implement the point→class bands; load gene-specific rule specs from ClinGen CSpec (start with F8/F9, VWF). Treat BA1 as a pre-filter (excluded from the Bayesian sum, per ClinGen).
- **Code/artifacts:** `rules/point_engine.py`, `rules/vcep_loader.py`, `rules/specs/` (F8, F9, VWF, ITGA2B, ITGB3 ...).
- **Illustrative pseudocode:**
  ```python
  # rules/point_engine.py
  STRENGTH_POINTS = {"PVS":8,"PS":4,"PM":2,"PP":1,"BS":-4,"BP":-1,"BVS":-8}  # base scale
  def classify(evidence: list[EvidenceCode], spec: VCEPSpec) -> Classification:
      pts = sum(spec.points_for(e) for e in evidence)   # spec may override strengths
      band = spec.band_for(pts)                          # P / LP / VUS / LB / B
      return Classification(points=pts, call=band, applied=evidence, spec_version=spec.version)
  ```
- **Expected outcome:** Reproduces ClinGen eRepo classifications for a held-out set of expert-curated bleeding-gene variants (target high concordance); every output carries its points + applied codes + spec version.

**Step 0.3 — Data infrastructure**
- **Objective:** Reliable access to reference data.
- **Goal:** Cached, versioned access to gnomAD (ancestry-resolved AFs), ClinVar, ClinGen eRepo, HPO, and bleeding-gene MAVE/functional datasets where available.
- **Methods:** Download/adapter scripts with checksums and provenance logging.
- **Code/artifacts:** `data/sources/*.py`, `data/manifest.json` (versions, dates, checksums).
- **Expected outcome:** `make data` produces a verified, provenance-logged local store.

### Phase 1 — Evidence orchestration layer (Months 2–5)

**Step 1.1 — Per-code evidence adapters**
- **Objective:** Populate the points ledger automatically.
- **Goal:** One adapter per evidence code (or code group) over the open ecosystem.
- **Methods:** gnomAD ancestry-aware AF → PM2/BS1; calibrated AlphaMissense/REVEL (Pejaver-style thresholds) → PP3/BP4 at the *correct strength*; autoPVS1 → PVS1; literature mining (AutoPM3-style RAG) → PM3/PS1/PS4; ClinVar prior submissions; splice predictor (per current VCEP cutoffs) → PP3/BP4/BP7 and splicing context.
- **Code/artifacts:** `adapters/gnomad.py`, `adapters/insilico_calibrated.py`, `adapters/autopvs1.py`, `adapters/litmine_pm3.py`, `adapters/clinvar.py`, `adapters/splice.py`.
- **Expected outcome:** For a test variant, the system auto-assembles a points ledger that matches an expert curator's applied codes on a benchmark set (report per-code precision/recall).

**Step 1.2 — Candidate belief-state adapters (prioritizers)**
- **Objective:** Ingest upstream diagnosis/prioritization.
- **Goal:** Consume Exomiser/AI-MARRVEL (and optionally DeepRare) output as the candidate-variant belief state.
- **Methods:** Standardize their outputs into a common `CandidateSet` schema; record disagreement.
- **Code/artifacts:** `adapters/exomiser.py`, `adapters/aimarrvel.py`, `core/candidate_set.py`.
- **Expected outcome:** End-to-end: VCF + HPO → ranked candidates with an attached, rule-grounded points ledger per candidate.

### Phase 2 — Decision & orchestration engine (the novel core) (Months 4–8)

**Step 2.1 — Evidence-gap analysis**
- **Objective:** Compute the gap between current and threshold, and what could close it.
- **Goal:** For each candidate VUS, enumerate *attainable* missing codes and their Δpoints.
- **Methods:** Given the ledger and the gene's VCEP spec, determine which not-yet-applied codes are biologically/clinically attainable for this variant/patient (e.g., PS2 attainable only if parents available; PS3-splice attainable if variant near splice region; PP1 attainable if informative family exists), and the points each would add.
- **Code/artifacts:** `engine/gap.py` (`attainable_codes(ledger, spec, patient) -> list[CodeOpportunity{code, delta_points, prerequisites}]`).
- **Illustrative pseudocode:**
  ```python
  def gap_to_target(ledger, spec, target="LP"):
      need = spec.band_threshold(target) - ledger.points
      opps = attainable_codes(ledger, spec, patient)      # each has delta_points + prerequisites
      return need, opps
  ```
- **Expected outcome:** For each VUS, a list of "what would move it and by how much," validated against ClinGen's functional-evidence-potential logic.

**Step 2.2 — Code → action mapping**
- **Objective:** Translate evidence opportunities into orderable real-world actions.
- **Goal:** A catalog mapping each code opportunity to concrete assays/tests/modalities, with cost, turnaround, and yield priors (domain pack).
- **Methods:** Build the bleeding-disorders action catalog: PS3→{factor activity, VWF:FVIII binding, LTA, CD41/CD61 flow cytometry, clot retraction, thrombin generation}; PS3-splice→{RNA-seq, RT-PCR, minigene}; PS2→{parental/trio testing}; PP1→{family segregation}; plus whole-modality escalations {long-read genome, optical mapping, methylation}. Priors seeded from literature, versioned.
- **Code/artifacts:** `engine/action_catalog/bleeding.yaml`, `engine/action_map.py`.
- **Expected outcome:** Each code opportunity resolves to ≥1 concrete action with attached cost/turnaround/yield priors.

**Step 2.3 — Value-of-information ranking & recommendation**
- **Objective:** Recommend the best next action.
- **Goal:** Rank actions by expected information gain (expected Δpoints toward a confident call, weighted by assay sensitivity/yield) per dollar and per week; return a ranked, explained recommendation with calibrated confidence.
- **Methods:** EIG/expected-points-gain under the assay's sensitivity prior; cost- and time-normalized utility; risk-aversion option; tie to a clear "expected post-action classification" statement.
- **Code/artifacts:** `engine/voi.py` (`rank_actions(opps, action_map, priors) -> RankedPlan`), `engine/recommend.py`.
- **Expected outcome:** For a VUS, output of the form: *"Recommended next: VWF:FVIII binding assay (~$X, ~2 wks); expected to add PS3 (Strong, +4) → likely Pathogenic; confidence Y. Alternative: family segregation (PP1)."* Beats greedy/random baselines in simulation.

### Phase 3 — Equity module (Months 6–9)

**Step 3.1 — Ancestry inference + evidence-reliability flags**
- **Objective:** Make the engine ancestry-aware.
- **Goal:** Infer genetic-similarity group from VCF; flag/down-weight PM2/PP3 reliability when reference data are sparse for that group.
- **Methods:** Standard ancestry inference (reference-panel projection); reliability scoring of AF/in-silico evidence by group-specific data density.
- **Code/artifacts:** `equity/ancestry.py`, `equity/reliability.py`.
- **Expected outcome:** Per-variant reliability annotations on PM2/PP3; documented behavior on under-represented-ancestry test cases.

**Step 3.2 — Equitable-evidence routing + reporting**
- **Objective:** Reduce ancestry disparities in resolution.
- **Goal:** Bias action recommendations toward ancestry-robust evidence; produce equity dashboards.
- **Methods:** Modify VOI scoring to prefer functional/segregation actions when biased-code reliability is low; per-case and cohort equity metrics.
- **Code/artifacts:** `equity/routing.py`, `equity/dashboard.py`.
- **Expected outcome:** In ancestry-stratified evaluation, narrower gap in resolution rate vs. the non-equity-aware configuration.

### Phase 4 — Whole-odyssey (cross-modality) layer (Months 8–11)

**Step 4.1 — Case-level state & modality escalation**
- **Objective:** Lift the engine from single-variant to whole-case.
- **Goal:** Model the *case* belief state and recommend whole-modality next steps (RNA-seq, long-read, optical mapping, methylation, proteomics), reanalysis timing, and matchmaking submission.
- **Methods:** Case-level decision policy over the modality action space; "detection vs. interpretation" failure-mode routing (missing-second-variant / hard-to-sequence region → long-read/optical; suspected splicing/expression → RNA-seq; suspected imprinting → methylation).
- **Code/artifacts:** `engine/case_policy.py`, `engine/odyssey_actions.py`.
- **Expected outcome:** For an unsolved case, a ranked whole-workup plan (not just per-variant), validated against retrospective journey cohorts.

### Phase 5 — Human-in-the-loop UI, audit & learning loop (Months 9–13)

**Step 5.1 — Reasoning-trace review UI**
- **Objective:** Clinician-facing decision support.
- **Goal:** Show the points ledger, the gap, the ranked recommendations, and full provenance; capture accept/override.
- **Methods:** Web UI (lightweight; the design system follows the frontend skill when built); every element links to its evidence source and VCEP rule version.
- **Code/artifacts:** `ui/` (review app), `api/main.py` (service), `core/audit.py` (immutable, hash-chained log).
- **Expected outcome:** A geneticist can review a case, see *why* a variant is a VUS and the cheapest path out, and accept/override — all logged.

**Step 5.2 — Verifiable learning loop**
- **Objective:** Improve from use.
- **Goal:** Feed human accept/override + realized assay outcomes back into cost/yield/attainability priors — auditably.
- **Methods:** Outcome store; per-institution prior updating; every update is attributable to specific cases (no opaque retraining of the verdict logic).
- **Code/artifacts:** `learn/outcome_store.py`, `learn/prior_update.py`.
- **Expected outcome:** Demonstrable, auditable improvement in recommendation quality as outcomes accumulate (shown in simulation and on retrospective data).

### Phase 6 — Validation (Months 11–15)

**Step 6.1 — Core ablation (headline)**
- **Objective:** Prove the decision layer's added value.
- **Goal:** Full system vs. underlying-tools-only, on identical cohorts.
- **Methods:** Paired comparison; metrics = VUS resolution rate, Δpoints, cost/time per resolved case, concordance with VCEP truth; appropriate paired statistics (McNemar/Wilcoxon/DeLong) with effect sizes and multiple-comparison correction.
- **Code/artifacts:** `eval/ablation.py`, `eval/stats.py`.
- **Expected outcome:** Pre-registered, honestly-reported demonstration that the layer improves resolution and/or cost — including any negative results.

**Step 6.2 — Retrospective journey + simulation + calibration/equity**
- **Objective:** External and stress validation.
- **Goal:** Match recommended vs. actually-resolving actions (retrospective); beat greedy/random (simulation); reliability diagrams (calibration); ancestry-stratified gaps (equity).
- **Methods:** Solve-RD/RD-Connect/UDN + bleeding registries; the parametric simulator; calibration and equity analyses.

## Appendix A — Glossary (selected)

- **VUS** — Variant of Uncertain Significance; formally, a variant whose evidence points fall in the uncertain band.
- **ACMG/AMP + ClinGen point system** — quantitative, Bayesian-calibrated variant-classification framework; evidence codes carry points at Supporting/Moderate/Strong/Very-Strong strengths.
- **VCEP / CSpec** — ClinGen Variant Curation Expert Panel; CSpec = the registry of gene-specific rule specifications.
- **PS3/BS3** — strong functional evidence for/against pathogenicity.
- **VOI / EIG** — value of information / expected information gain; the decision-theoretic basis for ranking next actions.
- **MAVE** — Multiplexed Assay of Variant Effect (saturation functional data).
